Public Finance Review 3 (Externalities)

Externalities

  • Definition: An activity on one entity that affects the welfare of another entity in a way that is outside the market mechanism.
  • Nature of Externalities
    • Externalities can be produced by consumers as well as firms.
    • Externalities are reciprocal in nature.
    • Externalities can be positive
    • Public Goods can be viewed as a special kind of externality.
  • Graphical Analysis

  • Remarks:
    • The marginal private cost reflects payments made by B for productive input and is assumed here to increase with output.
    • The marginal damage inflicted on L by the pollution at each level of output is denoted by MD, it;s drawn sloping upward, reflecting the assumption that as Lisa is subjected to additional pollutionm she become worse off at an increasing rate.
    • The curve labeled MB indicates the marginal benefit to B of each level of output. It is assumed to decline as output increases.
    • For society’s point of view, production occurs as long as the marginal soical benifit exceeds the marginal social cost.
    • Efficiency from a society’s point of view requires $MSC = MB$, responds to the intersection of two schedules.

  • Remarks
    • When output falls from $Q_1$ to $Q^*$, Bart loses area dcg in profits. However, the reduction in Bart’s output increases Lisa’s welfare by area cdhg (equals to abfe). Thus, the net gain to society is area dhg.
  • Bargaining and Coase Theorem
    • There is an argument that the root cause of inefficiency associated with externalities is the absence of property right.
    • When the property rights are assigned, individuals may respond to the externality by bargaining with each other.
    • Provided that transaction costs are negligible, an efficient solution to an externality problem is achieved as long as someone is assigned property rights, independent of who is assigned those rights. In other words, the costs to the parties of bargaining are low and the owners of resources can identify the source of damages to their property and legally prevent damages
    • Pareto optimum can be achieved via private negotiation regardless of who owns the property right.
    • When the property right is assigned to B, to prevent damage, when $MD > MB-MPC$, that is the payment L willing to give exceeds B’s non-produce. At point $Q$, $MB-MPC = 0$, while $MD > 0$. Hence, there is a scope for a bargain.
    • To the left of $Q^*$, $MD < MB-MPC$, L is not willing to pay.
    • the total payment will be at least dcg and at most cdhg.
  • Pigouvian Tax
    • A tax levied on each unit of an externality-generator’s output in an amount equal to the marginal damage at the efficient level of output.

  • Pigou Subsidy
    • A subsidy given by government to the externality-generator before $Q_1$ in the amount equal to the marginal damage at the efficient level of output. the externality-generator will receives a subsidy dfhc.
